Marcus Porcius Cato

Cato the Elder, farmer made censor, first to write history in Latin.

Timeline of the events in the life of this old roman senator:

  • 234 B.C.: Born in Tusculum in the Sabine Mountains.
  • 214 B.C.: Serves at Capua during the Second Punic War, being a military tribune at the young age of 20.
  • 207 B.C.: Fights and render vital services during the Battle of Metaurus, where Hasdrubal Barca was slain.
  • 205-204 B.C.: Appointed Quaestor, escorted the baggage ships of Scipio Africanus Major from Sicily to Africa.
  • 195 B.C.: Elected junior consul, enacts the first two of the Porcian Laws.
  • 194 B.C.: Returns to Rome after the subjection of the province of Hispania Citerior, where he claims he destroyed more cities than the days he spent in the land. There he also coined the phrase bellum se ipsum allet, "War feeds itself".
  • 191 B.C.: Participates in the Battle of Thermopylae as lieutenant-general, which led to the downfall of Antiochus III the Great.
  • 157 B.C.: After visiting Carthage as a deputy to arbitrate a dispute between the Carthanigians and Massinissa, king of Numidia. After seeing the prosperity of the city, Cato becomes convinced of the necessity of the annihilation. It is from this point forward that Cato ends every single speech with the phrase Ceterum censeo Carthaginem esse delendam, "moreover, I advise that Carthage must be destroyed".
  • 149 B.C.: Cato the Elder dies at the age of 85, three years before the final defeat and destruction of Carthage. He was revered by peers and later romans for his austere discipline and adherence to the old roman virtues.
